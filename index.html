Absolute vs. Relative Position Encoding

Vanilla (unscaled) attention mechanism $$ \begin{aligned} e_{ij} &= x_i \Wq (x_j {\Wk})^\trans \\ \alpha_{ij} &= exp(e_{ij}) / \sum_k exp(e_{ik}) \\ y_i &= \sum_j \alpha_{ij} x_j \Wv \end{aligned} $$

absolute encoding
$$ \begin{aligned} e_{ij} &= (x_i \redpluspi) \Wq ((x_j \redpluspj) {\Wk})^\trans \\ y_i &= \sum_j \alpha_{ij} (x_j \redpluspj) \Wv \end{aligned} $$
relative encoding
$$ \begin{aligned} e_{ij} &= (x_i \Wq \greypluspijq) (x_j {\Wk} \redpluspijk)^\trans \\ y_i &= \sum_j \alpha_{ij} (x_j \Wv \redpluspijv) \end{aligned} $$

Recent Related Work

Time2Vec: Learning a Vector Representation of Time 📝

Goal: general model-agnostic plug-and-play feature

Inspiration: prior work 📝 decomposed the values of 1-dim time series into several sine functions plus a linear function to extra- or interpolate the given signal

Time2Vec: Learning a Vector Representation of Time 📝

Method: decompose time signal with periodic functions

\[ \tau_i(t) = \begin{cases} \omega_i t + \phi_i & \text{if } i = 0 \\ f(\omega_i t + \phi_i) & \text{else} \end{cases}, \quad\text{for}\quad i = 1, \dots, d_{feat} \]

$\omega_i$ and $\phi_i$ are learnable

$f$ is a periodic function, $sin$ worked best, benchmarked against $mod$, $triangle$, and non-periodic ones (very bad)

$\tau$ is passed as a separate input to the model
instead of adding it to other embeddings

concatenation instead of aggregation increases input dimension

Fourier Features Let Networks Learn High Frequency Functions in Low Dimensional Domains 📝

Goal: solve several CV tasks with MLPs + Fourier features

Inspiration: prior work 📝 & 📝 has experimentally found that a heuristic sinusoidal mapping of input coordinates allows MLPs to represent higher frequency content

Fourier Features Let Networks Learn High Frequency Functions in Low Dimensional Domains 📝

Method: special Fourier mapping that yields a stationary neural tangent kernel with tuneable bandwidth

\[ \tau_i(t) = \begin{cases} a_i cos(\textcolor{grey}{2\pi} b_i^\trans t) & \text{if } i \text{ is odd} \\ a_i sin(\textcolor{grey}{2\pi} b_i^\trans t)& \text{else} \end{cases} , \quad\text{for}\quad i = 1, \dots, d_{feat} \\ \text{with}\quad t \in \RR^{l_{patch}}, a_i \in \RR,\ b_i \in \RR^{l_{patch}} \]

$a_i$ and $b_i$ are learnable

but here it can be added to the other embeddings (unspecified)

$t \in \RR^{l_{patch}}$ requires a different feature embedding,
resulting in patch-position-depended frequencies $b_{ij}$

same time point $t_j$ is encoded differently depending on its position in the patch
